The problem

The prices of fresh fruits and vegetables in northern communities are inordinate, due to the costs of transporting goods from the south. On average, a weekly supply of groceries for northern Canadians would cost 2-4 times the national average cost for groceries. Families in these areas currently suffer from nutrient deficiencies, as they are forced to purchase convenience products that do not provide adequate nourishment.

Our solution

RejuveNorth aims to amend this by offering ReFresh, an environmentally-responsible greenhouse system, designed to introduce affordable fresh produce to areas where it was previously unobtainable. The innovative hydroponic system recycles carbon dioxide emissions back into the dome, increasing CO2 levels to accelerate photosynthesis and plant growth. The technology also collects and condenses water vapour to circulate through the hydroponic system, making the dome sustainable and efficient.

Our Mission

RejuveNorth is committed to addressing the pressing issue of food inaccessibility and unaffordability in Northern Canada.

Anaerobic Digestion

Anaerobic digestion is the decomposition process of organic matter by microorganisms to produce biogas: a renewable, environmentally-friendly fuel. In the dome, food waste enters the primary chamber where it is agitated in a slurry of hydrolytic, acetogenesis, and methanogenesis bacteria. The waste decomposes over a series of stages: hydrolysis, acidogenesis, acetogenesis, and methanogenesis; yielding carbon dioxide and methane. These gases are funnelled through a series of tubes until they reach the heat and power system. Here, the gases combust as the encounter extreme temperatures, yielding carbon dioxide and water. Nitrogen and oxygen are drawn into the area from the exterior of the dome to fuel the flame, while carbon dioxide and water vapour are sucked into another section of tubing. The water vapour is cooled, and collected into a 3000 gallon reservoir tank. This water will be stored, combined with nutrients, and pumped through the hydroponics system to nourish the maturing plants. The carbon dioxide is separated, and drawn with the help of fans back up into the dome where it is absorbed by the plants for photosynthesis.

CO2 sequestration

Natural gas is funnelled through a series of tubes until it reaches the heat and power system. Here, the gases combust as the encounter extreme temperatures, yielding carbon dioxide and water. Nitrogen and oxygen are drawn into the area from the exterior of the dome to fuel the flame, while carbon dioxide and water vapour are sucked into another section of tubing. The water vapour is cooled, and collected into a 3000 gallon reservoir tank. This water will be stored, combined with nutrients, and pumped through the hydroponics system to nourish the maturing plants. The carbon dioxide is separated, and drawn with the help of fans back up into the dome where it is absorbed by the plants for photosynthesis. The dome’s air is in constant circulation, as one fan pulls the cool air into the anaerobic digestion system and pushes it over the heated coil above the heat and power system. Once the air has reached a sufficient temperature, another fan draws the warm air back into the dome. It rises to crown of the dome, where it cools and gradually sinks down to the floor where it is once again heated. The decision was made to implement the anaerobic digestive system due to its numerous beneficial factors. The ReFresh uses the combusted gases produced from decomposed food waste to support the growth of new plants, ensuring that nothing truly goes to waste. By recycling and increasing the dome’s carbon dioxide levels, increased crop yields, plant growth, and rates of photosynthesis will be achieved, thus providing northern communities with nutrient-rich produce more quickly.

Water condensation and resuse

Our greenhouse has an incorporated carbon dioxide and water recycling system to make it as environmentally responsible and sustainable as possible. The anaerobic digestion system’s combustive process yields carbon dioxide and water. The water vapour is cooled and condensed by a dehumidifier, then collected in a 3000 gallon reservoir tank. Several air stones connected to a pump occupy the base of the tank, aerating the supply. A second pump rests in the water, pumping the it through a series of tubes into the hydroponics system once the necessary nutrients have been added. As it is a Nutrient Film Technique system, water flows gradually through the pipes at low levels. Unabsorbed or unevaporated water returns to the holding tank, where it combines with newly condensed water vapour and nutrients to be circulated through the system once more.

Hydroponics and Vertical Farming

We decided to grow our produce in a hydroponic system due to the numerous benefits it provides. By growing fruits and vegetables hydroponically in the north, we are able to grow fresh produce year-round in a controlled environment, unaffected by exterior permafrost, light and temperature variations. Hydroponic gardening occurs without the use of soil, and requires merely 1/20 of the amount of water used in traditional soil-based gardening. Hydroponics also provide a clean, sterile environment for plant growth, eliminating the need for pesticides. Plant growth is accelerated and production increased when grown hydroponically, and produce contains up to an additional 50% nutrient content compared to soil grown produce.

Superior materials

To construct our hydroponic system, we used two inch diameter ABS piping, elbows, couplings and caps as they are durable yet cost-efficient. We connected the pump in the reservoir tank to the rest of the system using three quarters of an inch diameter PVC tubing, selecting a smaller tube to help retain water pressure. We selected single bulbed grow lights to install in our system to ensure that all of the plants receive an adequate amount of light to support growth. We decided to transfer our plants into net pots once they leave the nursery, to protect the continuously developing roots and to provide the optimum conditions for nutrient absorption. To provide sufficient nutrients for plant growth, we selected a slow release fertilizer to add to the reservoir tank to be pumped through the system. We also used air stones, an air pump, and tubing to aerate the reservoir tank.

Geodesic dome

The ReFresh geodome design

RejuveNorth’s geodome design is specifically engineered to withstand the harsh conditions of the north. The sloped panels of the structure are designed increase the aerodynamic properties, and prevent snow accumulation. The walls are made of triangular, double layered, acrylic panels and are connected by galvanized steel supports. The interior of the panels are coated with an aluminum based paint, to reflect infrared light and retain heat.
